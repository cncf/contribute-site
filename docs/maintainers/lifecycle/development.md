title: Professional Development
description: Guidelines for professional development of maintainers

Maintainers are technical leaders, community operators, and governance stewards.
This page provides a practical development path for maintainers who want to
grow impact while reducing burnout risk.

## Development Areas

### Technical Leadership

- Improve review quality, not just review speed.
- Share architecture context in issues and pull requests.
- Delegate ownership of subsystems to grow reviewer depth.

See:

- [Reviewing Guide](/projects/best-practices/templates/reviewing/)
- [Issue Labels](/projects/best-practices/templates/issue-labels/)

### Community Leadership

- Build onboarding pathways for first-time contributors.
- Create clear ways to contribute beyond code.
- Run regular community touchpoints with notes and follow-ups.

See:

- [Contributing Guide](/projects/best-practices/templates/contributing/)
- [Contributor Growth Framework](/projects/best-practices/community/contributor-growth/)
- [Recruiting Playbook](/projects/best-practices/community/recruiting-playbook/)

### Governance and Program Leadership

- Keep governance documentation accurate to current practice.
- Keep maintainer selection and transition processes documented.
- Use transparent, repeatable decision-making.

See:

- [Governance Overview](/projects/best-practices/governance/)
- [Maintainer Council Template](/projects/best-practices/governance/templates/governance-maintainer/)
- [Leadership Selection](/projects/best-practices/governance/leadership-selection/)

## 30-60-90 Development Plan

### First 30 days

- Document current maintainer duties and escalation paths.
- Audit review queue and identify top bottlenecks.
- Identify one contributor to mentor on reviews.

### Days 31-60

- Publish or update contributor onboarding docs.
- Define ownership areas for maintainers.
- Start one recurring project-health review rhythm.

### Days 61-90

- Delegate at least one recurring maintainer responsibility.
- Propose one governance improvement from observed friction.
- Share progress and next goals in a public project update.

## Suggested Annual Goals

- Improve contributor retention and repeat contribution rate.
- Reduce dependency on a small set of maintainers.
- Improve review turnaround and issue triage consistency.
- Increase clarity of governance and role expectations.
