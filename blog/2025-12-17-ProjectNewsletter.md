## ğŸ³ Docker Hardened Images free for projects

Today, Docker announced that they are making Docker Hardened Images free to all developers.

DHI provides maintainers of CNCF projects with secure, minimal, continuously maintained, base container images that reduce vulnerabilities and attack surfaces while fitting into existing workflows and improving supply-chain security overall.

Many CNCF projects can already be found in the DHI catalog, and giving the broader community access to secure, well-maintained building blocks helps us strengthen the software supply chain together.

[Learn how to get started in this announcement from Docker](https://www.docker.com/blog/docker-hardened-images-for-every-developer/).

## ğŸ“ˆ Telemetry collection and privacy guidelines

The Linux Foundation maintains [telemetry](https://www.linuxfoundation.org/legal/telemetry-data-policy) and [privacy](https://www.linuxfoundation.org/legal/privacy-policy) policies to help protect projects and their users. Projects are expected to follow these guidelines, and for CNCF-provided services staff also reviews third-party tools to ensure alignment before making them available to projects.

We've identified a small number of cases where third-party services may not yet fully align, and we'll be reaching out to work with those project teams to get that sorted. Our goal is to be supportive and collaborative, and to help maintainers ensure transparency, user trust, and alignment with community expectations.
